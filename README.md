# WNBA Draft Board Model

A data-driven WNBA draft tool that ranks NCAA women's basketball players by their fit for specific WNBA teams. Built by Derek Yee & Vedanth Chamala (team needs model) and Evan & Jenna (player archetypes & ranking).

---

## What It Does

For each WNBA team on the clock, the model answers: **"Which available NCAA players best address this team's specific weaknesses?"**

It combines two signals:
- **WNBA Readiness Score** вЂ” how good is this player overall, trained on real WNBA career outcomes
- **Team Fit Score** вЂ” how well do this player's strengths address what this specific team lacks

The final ranking is `55% readiness + 45% fit` (adjustable in the app).

## How the Model Works

### Step 1 вЂ” Multi-Year NCAA Scraping
`01_scrape_multi_year.py` scrapes all D1 women's team and player stats from sports-reference.com for 2022вЂ“2025, looping over all 31 conferences. Checkpoint-safe: skips years whose output files already exist.

`01b_scrape_draft_class_only.py` scrapes the college stats of historical WNBA draft picks (2018вЂ“2024), used as ground-truth training data for the readiness model.

### Step 2 вЂ” Physical Measurables
`02_scrape_measurables.py` fetches each player's profile page to extract height and weight. Height is the strongest single predictor of WNBA role вЂ” without it, players like a guard who blocks shots get misclassified as bigs. Resume-safe.

### Step 3 вЂ” Opponent Strength
`03_opp_strength.py` computes opponent-adjusted strength metrics for each team's schedule. These feed into the feature build to give context to raw stats: a 20 PPG season against a weak conference means less than 18 PPG against the SEC.

### Step 4 вЂ” Multi-Year Feature Aggregation
`04_build_features.py` collapses per-year data into one row per player using **exponential recency weighting combined with minutes weighting**:

```
weight = exp(0.5 Г— (year в€’ 2022)) Г— minutes_played
```

A player's 2025 season counts ~4.5Г— more than their 2022 season, and a 35 mpg season counts more than a 5 mpg cameo. The script also computes:

- **Season trend** вЂ” slope of BPM and PPG over time (rewards improving players)
- **Derived features** вЂ” AST/TOV ratio, three-point rate, inside scoring composite, defensive composite
- **Transfer handling** вЂ” when a player appears on multiple teams in one year, the highest-MPG row is kept

### Step 5 вЂ” WNBA Archetype Clustering
`05_scrape_wnba_archetypes.py` scrapes WNBA player season stats from basketball-reference and clusters WNBA players into archetypes using **Gaussian Mixture Models (GMM)**. These cluster labels become ground-truth training signal for step 6.

**Why cluster WNBA players first?** WNBA archetypes don't come pre-labeled. By clustering on actual WNBA stats, we get role labels that reflect what players *became* in the pros вЂ” not what they looked like in college. Step 6 then learns which college stats + height predict those pro roles.

`05b_wnba_team_needs.py` scrapes 2025 WNBA team stats to compute each team's statistical weaknesses for the fit score.

| Cluster | Archetype | Key stats |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| вЂ” | **Primary Creator** | High USG%, PPG, shot creation |
| вЂ” | **Balanced Contributor** | Moderate across all categories |
| вЂ” | **Interior Defender** | High BLK, REB, interior anchoring |
| вЂ” | **3&D Wing** | High 3P%, STL, low USG% |
| вЂ” | **Playmaker** | High AST%, low USG%, role player |
| вЂ” | **Stretch Big** | High 3P% + REB combination |

### Step 6 вЂ” Archetype Classifier
`06_archetype_classifier.py` trains a **logistic regression classifier** on the WNBA archetype labels from step 5, using pre-draft college stats + height as features. The trained classifier assigns each current prospect a predicted WNBA archetype.

**Why logistic regression?**
- Coefficients are directly interpretable: which college stats push a player toward each archetype.
- Regularized (L2) to handle the small training set (~150вЂ“200 matched players).
- Calibrated probabilities useful for soft/uncertain assignments.
- Height disambiguates statistical profiles that look similar across positions.

### Step 7 вЂ” XGBoost Readiness Model
`07_xgboost_model.py` trains a **gradient boosting model** (sklearn's `HistGradientBoostingRegressor`) to output a 0вЂ“100 **WNBA readiness score**.

**Key improvement over the old Ridge approach:** The previous model used a hand-crafted synthetic target derived from the input features themselves. The gradient boosting model instead trains directly on **real WNBA career performance labels** from 2018вЂ“2024 draft picks, making it actual prediction rather than proxy scoring.

**Training pipeline:**
1. Match 2018вЂ“2024 draft picks to their pre-draft NCAA stats
2. Build a composite WNBA success score (career PER, WS/48, games played)
3. Train with leave-one-year-out cross-validation
4. Score current 2025 prospects

**Why gradient boosting over Ridge?**
- Ridge needed a synthetic target because it had no real WNBA labels. Gradient boosting learns from ground truth.
- Captures nonlinear interactions (e.g., high usage only matters when efficiency is high).
- `HistGradientBoostingRegressor` handles missing values natively.
- Built-in feature importance shows which NCAA stats actually predict WNBA success.

Training set note: ~6 draft years Г— ~33 picks/year в‰€ 200 labeled players. Max depth=3, early stopping, and L2 regularization to avoid overfitting.

### Step 8 вЂ” PlayerвЂ“Team Fit Score
`08_fit_scores.py` bridges WNBA team weaknesses (from step 5b) to NCAA player strengths.

**WNBA team needs** come from step 5b: for each statistical feature correlated with winning, each team gets a `weighted_deficit` вЂ” how far below league average they are, scaled by that stat's importance to winning.

**WNBA в†’ NCAA stat mapping:**

| WNBA Team Need | NCAA Player Stats Used | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| `PTS_per_100` | pts_per_g, usg_pct, obpm | High |
| `AST_per_100` | ast_per_g, ast_pct | High |
| `DRB_per_100` | dreb_per_g, dreb_pct, treb_pct | High |
| `TS.` | ts_pct, efg_pct | High |
| `opp_FG%_per_game` | blk_per_g, blk_pct, dbpm | Medium (proxy) |
| `opp_3FG%_per_game` | stl_per_g, stl_pct, dbpm | Medium (proxy) |

> **Note on defensive stats:** Opponent stats are team-level metrics with no clean individual NCAA equivalent. Fit scores for teams with primarily defensive needs will be noisier than for teams with offensive needs.

**Fit score formula:**
```
fit_score(player, team) = ОЈ over needs:
    team_deficit[need] Г— ОЈ (player_z[ncaa_stat] Г— stat_weight Г— confidence)
```

**Final combined score:**
```
total_score = 0.55 Г— z(readiness_score) + 0.45 Г— z(fit_score)
```

The 55/45 split is adjustable in the React app via a slider.

## The React Webapp

The app has three views:

**Team View** вЂ” click any team to see:
- Their top statistical weaknesses (from the needs model)
- All available players ranked by fit score for that team
- Filters by archetype and position
- Click a player row to expand detailed stats (including Readiness score)
- "Draft" button to mark a player as taken (persisted to localStorage)

**Draft Board** вЂ” log of all drafted players with undo support.

**Mock Draft** вЂ” simulate a full draft order.

### Fit score display
When `prepare_data.py` has been run with the full pipeline output, the "Fit" column uses the pre-computed model score (0вЂ“100). Without the pipeline, it falls back to an archetype-based formula using `archetype_score` and `rank_in_archetype`.

## Key Design Decisions

**Why real WNBA labels over a synthetic Ridge target?** The old model hand-crafted a target from the input features themselves (CV RВІ was high but trivially so). The XGBoost model trains on actual career outcomes, so the score reflects what actually predicts WNBA success.

**Why cluster WNBA players before classifying prospects?** Pre-labeling archetypes on what players *became* in the WNBA is more meaningful than clustering on college stats. A player who looks like a wing in college might become a point guard or a stretch big вЂ” the WNBA clustering captures the real destination roles.

**Why exponential weighting and not just the most recent season?** A freshman's sophomore improvement trajectory is signal. A player who was great for 3 years then had an injury year should not be penalized as heavily. Recency weighting captures both.

**Why is the fit score 45% of the total?** A player who perfectly addresses one team's needs but is a mediocre prospect overall is still a bad pick. The readiness score (55%) prevents the model from recommending weak players just because their archetype fits. The ratio is tunable in the app.

**Why `weighted_deficit` from the needs model rather than raw deficits?** The weighted deficit incorporates how important each stat is to winning (via correlation weights). A team's rebounding deficit is automatically discounted if rebounding is less predictive of wins than scoring efficiency.
